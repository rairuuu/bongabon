BONGABON

Bongabon is a municipality in the province of Nueva Ecija in the Philippines. It is located in the northern part of the province and is bounded by the Sierra Madre Mountains to the east. Bongabon has a population of approximately 57,000 people, with Tagalog being the most widely spoken language. The town is known for its agriculture and is a major producer of rice, corn, vegetables, and fruits. The area also has several tourist attractions, including waterfalls, hot springs, and scenic hiking trails in the nearby mountains.

Bongabon is a municipality in the province of Nueva Ecija, Philippines, located in the Central Luzon region. It covers an area of approximately 370.3 square kilometers and is primarily composed of agricultural land with rice fields and vegetable gardens. Bongabon is divided into 33 barangays, which are the smallest administrative division in the Philippines.

MISSION and VISION

Mission

Enable the transformation of Bongabon into a peaceful and progressive municipality where; residents are united and progressively work to uplift the standards of living through the acquisition of knowledge and skills; effective and efficient local governance, linkages are in place; and all sectors of the society are cooperating with the local government towards development and progress.

Vision

Bongabon as an economically sound municipality, being the Onion Capital, is envisioned to be the Onion Research and Development Center of the Philippines equipped with stable and useful infrastructure with human-friendly ecosystem having healthy, safe, enlightened, and disciplined people serve by good government with highest integrity
